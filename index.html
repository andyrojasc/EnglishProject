Cybersecurity

What is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive infor mation; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

Implementing effective cybersecurity measures is particularly challenging today because there are more devices than people, and attackers are becoming more innovative.

Why is cybersecurity important?

In today’s connected world, everyone benefits from advanced cyberdefense programs. At an individual level, a cybersecurity attack can result in everything from identity theft, to extortion attempts, to the loss of important data like family photos. Everyone relies on critical infrastructure like power plants, hospitals, and financial service companies. Securing these and other organizations is essential to keeping our society functio- ning.

What is a hacker?

A hacker is someone who uses their knowledge in computing in order to gain unauthorized access to a system. A hacker is a person with very high computer skills and uses them in order to discover the vulnerabilities in a network or system, many times the hackers are paid by companies to discover their proneness to cyber attacks. The person who infiltrates a system with the intent of doing illegal activity is known as a cybercriminal.

Ransomware
Ransomware is a cyber attack that infects a computer with malicious software and encrypts all the data from the computer in exchange for money. The computer under attack is usually given a time frame and asks for a specific amount of money, once the person pays the amount of money asked it usually does not work, this due to the reason that once the data is encrypted it is nearly impossible to decrypt it.
Malware
Malware is a cyber attack that gains unauthorized access to a computer and affects the performance of the computer causing it damage, such as making the computer slow, overcharging the disk drive, etc. Some are also used to steal information but its main goal is to affect the computer's performance.
Social engineering
Social engineering is a cyber attack vector that relies heavily on human interaction and it often involves manipulating people into breaking normal security procedures and good practices in order to gain access to the system, networks or physical location, it can also be used for financial gain. Some types are vishing, scareware, baiting, and phishing.
Vishing
Vishing is a telephony’s version of phishing and uses voice messages to steal identities and financial resources. The term is a combination of "voice" and "phishing."
Scareware
Scareware is a type of malicious software that disguises itself as a normal website but in reality, it is infected with lots of malware. The main goal of spyware is to trick the user into clicking websites full of malicious software.
Baiting
Baiting is when an attacker leaves a malware-infected physical device, like a USB flash drive, in a place where it can be found by the victim.
Phishing
Phishing is an attack that consists of sending a lot of fraudulent emails disguised as a legitimate email. The email contains a malware inside of the message, therefore once the user clicks on the link all of their information will be stolen, such as passwords, login credentials, credit card info etc.
Spyware
Spyware is a type of malware that collects and shares information about a computer or network without the user’s consent.
Trojans
Trojans is a malware that is often disguised as legitimate software, this malware can delete data, block data, modify data and copy data by the instructions of a cybercriminal.
Worms
Worms are malware that doesn’t need a host program in order for them to run, self replicate and spread. Once a worm has made its way onto your system, usually via network it can then make multiple copies of itself and spread to other computers.
DoS
A denial of service attack is an attack meant to shut down a machine or network, making inaccessible to its intended users.
